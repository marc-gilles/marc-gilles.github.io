# Abstract
abstract: The world is full of odours, and many of them matter. Odours can signal the presence of food, disease, predators or potential partners, all of which is quite useful. The sense of smell, or olfaction, is one of the most ancient, found in all organisms from bacteria to plants and animals. Yet, there is one taxon widely believed to have little or sense of smell: birds. Why would birds be the only organisms that cannot smell?
